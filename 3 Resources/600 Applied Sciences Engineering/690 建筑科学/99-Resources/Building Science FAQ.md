# Building Science FAQ 常見問題

## 1. 「混凝土越強越好？」

**誤解**: Higher strength = better concrete.
**事實**: Strength is one property. Workability, durability, shrinkage, and cost are equally important. Over-specifying strength can increase cracking and cost unnecessarily.

## 2. 「鋼結構比混凝土更耐火？」

**誤解**: Steel is non-combustible, so it's fire-safe.
**事實**: Steel loses strength rapidly above 500°C. Concrete performs better in fire (insulating, slow heat transfer). Steel needs fireproofing (spray-applied, intumescent paint).

## 3. 「LEED 認證 = 綠建築？」

**誤解**: Certification equals sustainability.
**事實**: LEED is a checklist-based system. Real sustainability goes beyond points — operational performance matters more than certification. Certified buildings can still be energy hogs if poorly operated.

## 4. 「基礎越深越好？」

**誤解**: Deep foundations are always better.
**事實**: Foundation type depends on soil conditions, not preference. Shallow foundations on good soil outperform deep piles on bad soil. Over-design wastes money.

## 5. 「BIM 只是 3D 繪圖？」

**誤解**: BIM = 3D CAD.
**事實**: BIM is information management, not just 3D modeling. It integrates geometry, schedule, cost, sustainability, and operations data throughout the building lifecycle.

> 💡 The most expensive building is the one that doesn't serve its users. Function trumps form — always.
