# 05 契約論 / Contractarianism

Contractarian ethics grounds morality in the idea of a hypothetical agreement among rational
agents. Moral rules are those that free, equal, and rational persons would agree to.
契約論將道德建立在理性行動者之間的假設性契約之上。

## 社會契約傳統 / The Social Contract Tradition

| Thinker | Work | State of Nature | Key Idea |
|:--------|:-----|:----------------|:---------|
| **Thomas Hobbes** | *Leviathan* (1651) | "War of all against all" — solitary, poor, nasty, brutish, short | Rational self-interest → absolute sovereign for security |
| **John Locke** | *Second Treatise* (1689) | Peaceful but inconvenient — no impartial judge | Natural rights (life, liberty, property); government by consent |
| **Jean-Jacques Rousseau** | *The Social Contract* (1762) | Noble savage — natural pity and self-preservation | General will; "forced to be free" |
| **Immanuel Kant** | *Metaphysics of Morals* (1797) | — | Original contract as an idea of reason, not a historical event |

## 羅爾斯的無知之幕 / Rawls' Veil of Ignorance

John Rawls' *A Theory of Justice* (1971) revolutionized contractarian thinking:

### The Original Position 原初狀態
A hypothetical choice situation where rational agents select principles of justice behind
a **veil of ignorance 無知之幕** — they do not know:
- Their social class, race, gender, talents, or conception of the good
- Their generation or economic position

### The Two Principles of Justice 正義二原則
1. **Equal Basic Liberties 平等基本自由**: Each person has an equal right to the most extensive
   scheme of equal basic liberties compatible with similar liberties for all
2. **Difference Principle 差異原則**: Social and economic inequalities must (a) be attached to
   positions open to all under fair equality of opportunity, and (b) benefit the least advantaged

**Lexical Priority**: Principle 1 has absolute priority over Principle 2. Liberty cannot be
traded for economic gain.

### 為什麼選擇差異原則？ / Why the Difference Principle?
In the original position, rational agents use **maximin reasoning 最大化最小值策略**: given
uncertainty about your position in society, choose principles that make the worst-off position
as good as possible. You might be the one disadvantaged!

## 斯坎倫的契約主義 / Scanlon's Contractualism

T.M. Scanlon's *What We Owe to Each Other* (1998) shifts from self-interest to mutual
justification:

> "An act is wrong if its performance under the circumstances would be disallowed by any set of
> principles for the general regulation of behavior that no one could reasonably reject."

**Key Difference from Hobbesian Contractarianism**:
| Feature | Hobbesian Contractarianism | Scanlon's Contractualism |
|:--------|:--------------------------|:-------------------------|
| **Motivation** | Rational self-interest | Desire to justify oneself to others |
| **Foundation** | Prudential rationality | Reasonable rejection |
| **Moral realm** | Morality as mutual advantage | Morality as mutual respect |
| **Key concept** | What rational agents would accept | What no one can **reasonably reject** |

### Reasonable Rejection 合理拒絕
A principle can be reasonably rejected if there is an alternative that would impose fewer
burdens on those who would be most disadvantaged by it. The test is comparative, not absolute.

## 比較表 / Theory Comparison

| Theory | Basis of Obligation | What's Wrong with Wrongdoing? | Key Thinkers |
|:-------|:--------------------|:------------------------------|:-------------|
| **Hobbesian** | Rational self-preservation | Irrational — against your own interests | Hobbes, Gauthier |
| **Kantian** | Rational autonomy | Contradiction in will — you couldn't will the maxim as universal law | Kant |
| **Rawlsian** | Fairness | Principles the least advantaged could not reasonably accept | Rawls |
| **Scanlonian** | Mutual justifiability | Others could reasonably reject your principle | Scanlon, Darwall |

## David Gauthier's Morals by Agreement

Gauthier (*Morals by Agreement*, 1986) argues that morality emerges from rational bargaining
in prisoner's dilemma-like situations. Constrained maximization — cooperating conditional on
others cooperating — is more rational than straightforward maximization.

## 批判與回應 / Criticisms & Responses

| Criticism | Response |
|:----------|:---------|
| **Actual contracts matter, not hypothetical ones** (Dworkin) | Hypothetical contracts reveal what principles impartial rational agents would endorse |
| **Excludes non-contractors** (animals, future generations, disabled) | Rawls: justice as fairness is for the basic structure; we extend moral concern to others via different mechanisms |
| **Feminist critique** (Pateman, Okin) | The social contract tradition assumes male heads of household; needs reformulation |
| **Too abstract — what about real moral emotions?** | Contractualism is about justification, not motivation; we can have both |
