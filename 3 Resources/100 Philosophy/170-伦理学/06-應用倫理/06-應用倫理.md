# 06 應用倫理 / Applied Ethics

Applied ethics brings normative theories to bear on practical moral problems.
應用倫理將規範倫理理論應用於具體的道德實踐問題。

## 分支領域 / Branches of Applied Ethics

| Field | Key Questions | 核心問題 |
|:------|:--------------|:---------|
| Bioethics 生命倫理 | Abortion, euthanasia, genetic engineering, healthcare allocation | 墮胎、安樂死、基因工程、醫療資源分配 |
| AI Ethics AI倫理 | Autonomous weapons, algorithmic bias, machine moral status | 自主武器、演算法偏見、機器道德地位 |
| Environmental Ethics 環境倫理 | Climate justice, species extinction, anthropocentrism vs ecocentrism | 氣候正義、物種滅絕、人類中心主義 vs 生態中心主義 |
| Business Ethics 商業倫理 | Corporate responsibility, whistleblowing, fair wages | 企業責任、吹哨、公平工資 |
| Just War Theory 正義戰爭 | Jus ad bellum, jus in bello, humanitarian intervention | 開戰正義、戰中正義、人道干預 |

## 電車難題 / The Trolley Problem

The trolley problem (Philippa Foot, 1967; Judith Jarvis Thomson, 1976) is the classic
applied ethics thought experiment:

### Standard Case 標準案例
A runaway trolley is heading toward five people tied to the track. You can pull a lever
to divert it to a side track with one person tied there. Should you pull the lever?

### Footbridge Variant 天橋變體
You are on a footbridge above the track. The only way to stop the trolley and save five
is to push a large man off the bridge onto the track. Should you push him?

### 三大理論的回答 / Answers from Three Theories

| Theory | Switch (Pull Lever) | Footbridge (Push) | Reasoning |
|:-------|:-------------------:|:-----------------:|:----------|
| **Utilitarianism** | YES (5 > 1) | YES (5 > 1) | Only consequences matter; the numbers are the same |
| **Deontology (Kant)** | Possibly YES | NO | Push uses person as mere means; switch is redirecting a threat |
| **Virtue Ethics** | Depends on the agent | Depends on the agent | What would the virtuous person do? Compassion + practical wisdom |
| **Double Effect Doctrine** | YES (intend to save, foresee death) | NO (intend death as means) | Intention vs foresight distinction |

**Empirical Finding**: ~90% say pull the switch; ~10% say push. This asymmetry is the puzzle.
**Greene's dual-process explanation**: Personal harm (push) triggers emotional brain regions;
impersonal harm (switch) engages cognitive regions.

## 自主武器倫理 / Autonomous Weapons Ethics

LAWS (Lethal Autonomous Weapons Systems) raise urgent ethical questions:

| Issue | Description |
|:------|:------------|
| **Meaningful Human Control 有意義的人類控制** | Can we maintain human moral responsibility in the kill chain? |
| **Accountability Gap 問責缺口** | Who is responsible when an autonomous system violates IHL? — programmer? commander? no one? |
| **Martens Clause 馬爾頓斯條款** | Weapons must comply with "principles of humanity" and "dictates of public conscience" |
| **Discrimination 區分原則** | Can AI distinguish combatants from civilians reliably? |

**Arguments For a Ban**:
- Deontological: Machines cannot be moral agents; delegation of lethal authority violates human dignity
- Consequentialist: Risk of accidental escalation, proliferation, lower threshold for war

**Arguments Against a Ban**:
- LAWS might be *more* ethical than humans (no fear, anger, fatigue, revenge)
- The technology is inevitable; regulation > prohibition

## 生命倫理核心案例 / Bioethics Core Cases

### Abortion 墮胎
| Position | Core Argument | Thinkers |
|:---------|:--------------|:---------|
| **Pro-Life** | Fetus has a right to life from conception; personhood is biological | Marquis (future-like-ours argument) |
| **Pro-Choice** | Bodily autonomy; even if fetus is a person, it has no right to use another's body | Thomson (A Defense of Abortion, 1971 — violinist analogy) |
| **Moderate** | Moral status is gradual; early abortion permissible, late abortion restricted | Sumner, Steinbock |

### Euthanasia 安樂死
| Distinction | Description | Ethical Status |
|:------------|:------------|:---------------|
| Active vs Passive | Acting to cause death vs withholding treatment | Passive more widely accepted |
| Voluntary vs Non-voluntary | Patient's consent vs no consent possible | Voluntary is the key ethical requirement |
| Physician-Assisted Suicide | Doctor provides means; patient self-administers | Legal in some jurisdictions (e.g., Canada, Netherlands, Oregon) |

## 環境倫理 / Environmental Ethics

| Perspective | Core Claim | Thinkers |
|:------------|:-----------|:---------|
| **Anthropocentrism 人類中心** | Only humans have intrinsic value; nature valued instrumentally | Passmore |
| **Sentientism 感知主義** | All sentient beings have moral status | Singer, Bentham |
| **Biocentrism 生命中心** | All living things have intrinsic value | Taylor, Schweitzer |
| **Ecocentrism 生態中心** | Ecosystems, species, and the biosphere have moral standing | Leopold (Land Ethic), Naess (Deep Ecology) |

## 商業倫理 / Business Ethics

| Issue | Ethical Frameworks Applied |
|:------|:--------------------------|
| Whistleblowing 吹哨 | Deontology: duty to report wrongdoing; Utilitarianism: assess consequences |
| Stakeholder vs Shareholder | Friedman: maximize shareholder value vs Freeman: balance all stakeholders |
| Sweatshop Labor 血汗工廠 | Utilitarian: net benefit? Deontological: exploitation as means-only? |
| Corporate Personhood | Can corporations have moral responsibilities? |
