# 07 道德心理學 / Moral Psychology

Moral psychology is the empirical study of how humans actually make moral judgments,
develop moral capacities, and are motivated to act morally. 道德心理學是對人類道德判斷、
道德發展與道德動機的實證研究。

## 理性 vs 情感 / Reason vs Emotion

The central debate: Is moral judgment primarily rational or emotional?

### The Rationalist Tradition 理性主義傳統
| Thinker | Claim |
|:--------|:------|
| **Piaget** (1932) | Children develop moral reasoning through stages of cognitive development |
| **Kohlberg** (1969) | Moral judgment is cognitive — six stages of increasingly sophisticated reasoning |
| **Turiel** (1983) | Even young children distinguish moral rules (harm) from conventional rules (etiquette) |

### The Sentimentalist Tradition 情感主義傳統
| Thinker | Claim |
|:--------|:------|
| **Hume** (1739) | "Reason is, and ought only to be, the slave of the passions" |
| **Haidt** (2001) | Social Intuitionist Model — moral judgment is intuitive first, reasoning is post-hoc |
| **Prinz** (2007) | Moral judgments are emotional attitudes; reason provides factual inputs |

## 科爾伯格的道德發展階段 / Kohlberg's Stages of Moral Development

Lawrence Kohlberg (*Essays on Moral Development*, 1981) proposed six stages in three levels:

| Level | Stage | Description | Example Reasoning |
|:------|:------|:------------|:------------------|
| **Pre-Conventional 前習俗** | 1. Punishment/Obedience | Right = avoid punishment | "Don't steal — you'll go to jail" |
| | 2. Instrumental Exchange | Right = serve own interests | "Steal if you can get away with it" |
| **Conventional 習俗** | 3. Interpersonal Conformity | Right = be a "good" person | "Don't steal — what would people think?" |
| | 4. Law and Order | Right = obey laws, maintain social order | "Stealing is illegal and undermines society" |
| **Post-Conventional 後習俗** | 5. Social Contract | Right = uphold democratically agreed rules | "The law is usually right, but unjust laws may be broken" |
| | 6. Universal Ethical Principles | Right = self-chosen, universal principles | "Steal if a life is at stake — justice outweighs property" |

### Criticisms 批判
- **Gilligan's critique**: Kohlberg studied only males; women score lower because they use "care" reasoning rather than "justice" reasoning
- **Cultural bias**: Stages 5–6 reflect Western liberal individualism
- **Gender bias**: The justice-care distinction (see [[../09-比較倫理/09-比較倫理|09 比較倫理]])

## 格林雙重歷程理論 / Greene's Dual-Process Theory

Joshua Greene (*Moral Tribes*, 2013) uses fMRI data from trolley problem studies to argue for
a dual-process model:

```
Moral Judgment
     │
     ├── System 1: Automatic, Emotional, Fast
     │   └── Triggered by "personal" harm (up-close, physical)
     │   └── → Deontological judgments (don't push!)
     │
     └── System 2: Controlled, Cognitive, Slow
         └── Triggered by "impersonal" harm (detached, lever-pulling)
         └── → Utilitarian judgments (maximize lives saved!)
```

**Normative Implication**: Greene argues that deontological intuitions are emotional "alarms"
that misfire in novel contexts (like the footbridge). We should trust utilitarian reasoning
more in these artificial dilemmas.

### Key Studies
| Study | Finding |
|:------|:--------|
| Greene et al. (2001) | Personal moral dilemmas activate emotion-related brain areas (vmPFC, amygdala); impersonal activate working memory (DLPFC) |
| Greene et al. (2004) | Cognitive load selectively slows utilitarian judgments |
| Koenigs et al. (2007) | Patients with vmPFC damage make more utilitarian judgments |
| Paxton et al. (2012) | Reflective thinkers (high CRT scores) make more utilitarian judgments |

## 海特的社會直覺主義 / Haidt's Social Intuitionism

Jonathan Haidt's Social Intuitionist Model (*The Righteous Mind*, 2012):

**Core Claims**:
1. Moral judgments are **intuitive first**, reasoning second (the emotional dog wags the rational tail)
2. Reasoning is primarily a **post-hoc justification** process
3. Moral reasoning mainly serves to **persuade others**, not to discover truth
4. Moral change happens through **social influence**, not private reflection

### Moral Foundations Theory 道德基礎理論
Haidt identifies six innate moral "taste receptors":

| Foundation | Core Concern | Political Correlation |
|:-----------|:-------------|:----------------------|
| **Care/Harm** | Protect the vulnerable | Stronger in liberals |
| **Fairness/Cheating** | Reciprocal altruism, justice | Both, different interpretations |
| **Loyalty/Betrayal** | Group cohesion, patriotism | Stronger in conservatives |
| **Authority/Subversion** | Respect for tradition, hierarchy | Stronger in conservatives |
| **Sanctity/Degradation** | Purity, disgust | Stronger in conservatives |
| **Liberty/Oppression** | Resistance to domination | Both (different targets) |

## 同理心與道德 / Empathy and Morality

| Position | Claim | Thinkers |
|:---------|:------|:---------|
| **Empathy as Foundation** | Empathy is the basis of moral motivation; mirror neurons enable moral understanding | Hume, de Waal, Hoffman |
| **Empathy as Unreliable** | Empathy is biased (toward the near, similar, and cute); better to use impartial reasoning | Bloom (*Against Empathy*), Singer |
| **Empathy as Trainable** | Compassion training (as distinct from empathy) can expand moral concern | Ricard, Davidson, Buddhist psychology |

## 道德動機 / Moral Motivation

| Question | Debate |
|:---------|:------|
| **Internalism 內在論** | Must moral judgments necessarily motivate? |
| **Externalism 外在論** | Can one sincerely judge X is right and yet feel no motivation? |
| **Akrasia 意志薄弱** | Why do we knowingly do wrong? (see [[../08-後設倫理/08-後設倫理|08 後設倫理]]) |
| **Psychopathy** | Psychopaths make moral judgments without moral motivation — evidence for externalism? |
