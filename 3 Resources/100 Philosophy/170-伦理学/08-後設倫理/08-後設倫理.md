# 08 後設倫理 / Meta-Normative Theory

This chapter extends meta-ethics (see [[../01-元倫理學/01-元倫理學|01 元倫理學]]) by examining
the relationship between moral judgment and motivation, the supervenience of the moral on the
natural, and the particularism/generalist debate. 本章探討道德判斷與動機的關係、道德對自然的
隨附性，以及特殊主義與普遍主義之爭。

## 道德動機：內在論 vs 外在論 / Internalism vs Externalism

### Motivational Internalism 動機內在論
Sincere moral judgment necessarily motivates (at least to some degree).
真誠的道德判斷必然（至少在某種程度上）產生行動動機。

**Varieties 變體**:
| Type | Claim |
|:-----|:------|
| **Strong Internalism** | Moral judgment necessarily motivates all agents | 
| **Weak Internalism** | Moral judgment necessarily motivates rational agents |
| **Conditional Internalism** | Moral judgment motivates in psychologically normal agents |

**Key Arguments For 支持論證**:
1. **The Amoralist Challenge**: Can we conceive of someone who genuinely judges that X is right but feels absolutely no motivation? Internalists say no — this person doesn't really *understand* the moral judgment
2. **Humean Theory of Motivation**: Belief alone cannot motivate; desire is needed. Moral judgments must involve desire-like states (hence internalism)

### Motivational Externalism 動機外在論
Moral judgment does not necessarily motivate; motivation depends on contingent desires.
道德判斷不一定產生動機；動機取決於偶然的慾望。

**Key Arguments For 支持論證**:
1. **Psychopathy**: Psychopaths appear to make moral judgments (they can identify moral norms) but lack moral motivation. Externalists say: genuine judgment without motivation!
2. **Akrasia 意志薄弱**: Normal people routinely judge that X is right but fail to act — suggests judgment and motivation are separable
3. **Depression & Apathy**: Depressed individuals may retain moral judgments while losing all motivation

**Internalist Responses**:
- Psychopaths don't make *genuine* moral judgments; they use moral terms in an "inverted commas" sense (Hare)
- Akratic agents are motivated, but competing desires are stronger

### Comparison Table 比較表

| Criterion | Internalism | Externalism |
|:----------|:-----------:|:-----------:|
| Psychopathy evidence | Psychopaths don't make genuine moral judgments | Psychopaths make genuine moral judgments |
| Moral language learning | Must learn connection to motivation | Can learn truth-conditions independently |
| Hume's theory of motivation | Fits: moral judgments must be desire-like | Tension: how can pure belief motivate? |
| Akrasia | Motivation overridden, not absent | Shows judgment without motivation |

## 道德隨附性 / Moral Supervenience

The moral supervenes on the natural: no difference in moral properties without some difference
in natural properties. 道德屬性隨附於自然屬性之上：沒有自然屬性差異，就沒有道德屬性差異。

### Key Claims 核心主張
1. **Covariance 共變**: If two situations are identical in all natural respects, they must
   be identical in all moral respects
2. **No "Mixed Worlds"**: There cannot be a world exactly like ours in natural facts but
   with different moral facts (except by changing natural facts first)

### Why Supervenience Matters 為何重要
- **Moral Realism**: Supervenience seems to support naturalist realism — if moral facts
  supervene on natural facts, why not identify them?
- **Blackburn's Challenge**: The supervenience of moral on natural is itself a puzzling
  fact that non-cognitivists can explain better (as a constraint on our practices, not a
  metaphysical mystery)

### Jackson's Argument for Analytic Naturalism
Frank Jackson (*From Metaphysics to Ethics*, 1998):
1. Moral properties supervene on descriptive properties
2. For finite worlds, supervenience entails definability in descriptive terms
3. Therefore, moral properties are analytically reducible to descriptive properties

## 道德特殊主義 / Moral Particularism

Moral particularism denies that morality is codifiable into general principles.
道德特殊主義否認道德可以編碼為普遍原則。

### Dancy's Particularism
Jonathan Dancy (*Ethics Without Principles*, 2004):

**Core Claims**:
1. **Holism of Reasons 理由的整體論**: A feature that is a reason in one context may be
   no reason (or an opposite reason) in another context. Pleasure is usually good — but
   sadistic pleasure is bad.
2. **No Invariant Principles**: Because reasons are context-sensitive, there are no
   exceptionless moral principles
3. **Moral Judgment as Skill**: Moral judgment is more like perception than deduction
   — we see what is right in the particular case

### Generalism 普遍主義 (The Default View)
Most moral philosophers are generalists — they believe there are true moral principles:

| Type of Generalism | Claim | Thinkers |
|:-------------------|:------|:---------|
| **Absolute Principles** | Exceptionless principles (e.g., "Never lie") | Kant (arguably) |
| **Pro Tanto Principles** | Principles that count in favor but can be outweighed | Ross, most contemporary deontologists |
| **Ceteris Paribus Principles** | Principles that hold "other things being equal" | Many virtue ethicists |

### Particularism vs Virtue Ethics
Particularism echoes virtue ethics: the virtuous person doesn't consult a rulebook but
exercises *phronesis* in the particular case. But particularists go further — even the
virtues are not codifiable into exceptionless rules.

## 道德責任與意志薄弱 / Moral Responsibility & Akrasia

### Internalism and Responsibility
If (strong) internalism is true, how can we be held responsible for moral failure?
- If judgment necessarily motivates, and we fail to act, then we must have made a
  cognitive error — not a moral one
- Externalism preserves responsibility: we genuinely judge but lack motivation

### Nomy Arpaly on Akrasia
Arpaly (*Unprincipled Virtue*, 2003) argues akratic agents can be praised:
- Huck Finn believes he ought to turn in Jim (the runaway slave) but can't bring himself to do it
- Huck acts akratically — against his explicit moral judgment
- But Huck's akrasia reflects better moral *perception* than his explicit judgment

## 當代辯論 / Contemporary Debates

| Debate | Positions | Significance |
|:-------|:----------|:-------------|
| **Constitutivism** | Moral norms are constitutive of agency (Korsgaard, Velleman) | Grounds normativity in the structure of action |
| **Reasons Fundamentalism** | "Reason" is the primitive normative concept (Scanlon, Parfit) | Shifts focus from "good" and "right" to reasons |
| **Rationalism vs Sentimentalism** | Are moral requirements requirements of reason or of sentiment? | Connects to [[../07-道德心理學/07-道德心理學|07 道德心理學]] |
