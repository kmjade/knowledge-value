# 09 比較倫理 / Comparative Ethics

Comparative ethics examines moral traditions across cultures, exploring both convergence
and divergence in ethical thought. 比較倫理學探討不同文化中的道德傳統，尋找共通點與差異點。

## 東西方倫理比較 / East vs West Ethics

| Dimension | Western Ethics 西方倫理 | Eastern Ethics 東方倫理 |
|:----------|:-----------------------|:------------------------|
| **Foundation** | Reason, universal principles | Harmony, relationships, rituals |
| **Self** | Autonomous individual | Relational, interdependent self |
| **Key Question** | "What is the right act?" | "How should I cultivate myself?" |
| **Method** | Argument, analysis, justification | Practice, exemplars, self-examination |
| **Key Concepts** | Rights, justice, autonomy, utility | Ren 仁, Li 禮, Dao 道, Karuna 慈悲 |
| **Exemplars** | Kant, Mill, Rawls | Confucius, Mencius, Shantideva |

**Caveat**: These are broad characterizations, not essentialist claims. Both traditions are
internally diverse and have points of convergence.

## 儒家倫理 / Confucian Ethics

### 核心概念 / Core Concepts

| Concept | English | Description |
|:--------|:--------|:------------|
| **Ren 仁** | Benevolence, humaneness | The cardinal virtue — loving care for others; the ideal of human relations |
| **Li 禮** | Ritual propriety | Social norms, customs, rites that structure harmonious relationships |
| **Yi 義** | Righteousness | The disposition to do what is fitting in a given context |
| **Xiao 孝** | Filial piety | Reverence and care for parents and ancestors |
| **Junzi 君子** | Exemplary person | The ethical ideal — a person of cultivated virtue |

### Is Confucian Ethics a Virtue Ethics? 儒家倫理是否為德性倫理？

| Similarities to Virtue Ethics | Differences |
|:------------------------------|:------------|
| Focus on character development and cultivation 修養 | Less emphasis on individual flourishing vs social harmony |
| Central role of exemplars 君子 as moral model | More emphasis on ritual 禮 and social role performance |
| No rigid decision procedure | Stronger emphasis on family and hierarchical relationships |
| Phronesis-like practical wisdom | Ren is both particular and universal, but grounded in relationships |

**Scholarly Positions**:
- **"Confucian Virtue Ethics"**: P.J. Ivanhoe, Bryan Van Norden, May Sim — Confucius as Aristotle of the East
- **"Role Ethics"**: Roger Ames, Henry Rosemont — Confucian ethics is about role performance, not individual virtue
- **"Care Ethics" resonance**: Chenyang Li — Confucian ethics shares features with feminist care ethics

### Confucius vs Aristotle

| Dimension | Confucius 孔子 | Aristotle 亞里士多德 |
|:----------|:---------------|:---------------------|
| **Ultimate Goal** | Harmony (he 和) | Eudaimonia |
| **Method** | Ritual practice (li 禮), study, reflection | Habituation, phronesis, contemplation |
| **Emotions** | Cultivate appropriate emotions through ritual | Emotions as raw material for virtue |
| **Social Context** | Inherently relational — five relationships 五倫 | Polis — political community |
| **Self-Cultivation** | 修身 → 齊家 → 治國 → 平天下 | Individual virtue → political virtue |

## 佛教倫理 / Buddhist Ethics

### 核心原則 / Core Principles

| Concept | English | Description |
|:--------|:--------|:------------|
| **Karuna 慈悲** | Compassion | The wish for all beings to be free from suffering |
| **Metta 慈** | Loving-kindness | Unconditional goodwill toward all beings |
| **Ahimsa 不害** | Non-harm | The commitment to avoid causing suffering to any sentient being |
| **Pratityasamutpada 緣起** | Dependent origination | All phenomena arise interdependently — no independent self |
| **Upaya 方便** | Skillful means | Ethical action adapted to context and audience |

### Consequentialist or Virtue Ethics?
Buddhist ethics has elements of both:

| Consequentialist Elements | Virtue Ethics Elements |
|:--------------------------|:-----------------------|
| Centrality of suffering/well-being | Eightfold Path as character cultivation |
| Impartial compassion for all beings | Focus on intention (karma) and mental states |
| Bodhisattva maximizes benefit for all | Liberation (nirvana) as human flourishing |

**Key Difference**: Buddhist ethics is soteriologically oriented — aimed at liberation from
suffering rather than moral rightness per se.

### Engaged Buddhism 入世佛教
Thich Nhat Hanh and the Dalai Lama apply Buddhist ethics to contemporary problems:
- Environmental ethics (interdependence → ecological responsibility)
- Peace activism (ahimsa applied to war and conflict)
- Social justice (karuna demands structural change, not just individual compassion)

## 女性主義關懷倫理 / Feminist Ethics of Care

### Gilligan's Critique
Carol Gilligan (*In a Different Voice*, 1982) challenged Kohlberg's moral development theory:
- Kohlberg's highest stages privilege "justice reasoning" (rights, rules, impartiality)
- Women tend to use "care reasoning" (responsibility, relationships, context)
- The care perspective is not "lower" but *different* — an alternative moral orientation

### Nel Noddings' Care Ethics
Noddings (*Caring: A Feminine Approach to Ethics and Moral Education*, 1984):

**Core Claims**:
1. **The Caring Relation 關懷關係**: Ethics emerges from the natural caring response (mother-child paradigm)
2. **Engrossment & Motivational Displacement**: The carer attends to the cared-for and is moved by their needs
3. **The Ethical Ideal**: We cultivate an ideal of ourselves as caring persons
4. **Obligation**: We ought to care because caring sustains our ethical ideal

### Care Ethics vs Justice Ethics

| Dimension | Ethics of Care 關懷倫理 | Ethics of Justice 正義倫理 |
|:----------|:------------------------|:--------------------------|
| **Core Value** | Care, responsiveness, trust | Justice, fairness, rights |
| **Moral Agent** | Relational, embedded self | Autonomous, independent self |
| **Deliberation** | Contextual, narrative | Abstract, principled |
| **Key Question** | "How should I respond to this need?" | "What is the right thing to do?" |
| **Moral Emotion** | Empathy, compassion | Respect, indignation |
| **Thinkers** | Gilligan, Noddings, Tronto, Held | Kant, Rawls, Kohlberg |

### Broader Applications
- **Political Care Ethics**: Joan Tronto — care as a political concept; caring democracy
- **Global Care Ethics**: Virginia Held — care across borders; global responsibility
- **Integration Project**: Most care ethicists seek complementarity, not replacement, of justice ethics

## 跨傳統對話 / Cross-Traditional Dialogue

| Convergence | Example |
|:------------|:--------|
| **Compassion as central** | Confucian ren, Buddhist karuna, Christian agape, care ethics |
| **Welfare as relevant** | Utilitarianism, Buddhist ethics, basic in all traditions |
| **Virtue cultivation** | Aristotelian ethics, Confucian self-cultivation, Buddhist Eightfold Path |
| **The Golden Rule** | Present in virtually all major traditions |

**Divergence**:
- Western emphasis on rights vs Eastern emphasis on duties and roles
- Individual autonomy vs relational self
- Universal law vs contextual responsiveness
