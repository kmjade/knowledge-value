# 01 元倫理學 / Meta-Ethics

Meta-ethics is the branch of ethics that investigates the nature, meaning, and foundations
of moral claims. Rather than asking "What should I do?", it asks "What does it even mean
to say something is right or wrong?" 元倫理學不直接追問行為對錯，而是追問道德語言的本質。

## 核心問題 / Core Questions

| Question | Significance |
|:---------|:-------------|
| Are there objective moral truths? | 道德真理是否客觀存在？ |
| What does "good" mean? | 「善」的語義是什麼？ |
| Can we have moral knowledge? | 道德知識如何可能？ |
| Do moral statements express beliefs or emotions? | 道德陳述是信念還是情感表達？ |

## 認知主義 vs 非認知主義 / Cognitivism vs Non-Cognitivism

### Cognitivism 認知主義
Moral statements express beliefs that can be true or false.
道德陳述表達可為真假的信念。

**Varieties 分支**:
- **Moral Realism 道德實在論**: Moral facts exist mind-independently (e.g., naturalism, non-naturalism)
- **Error Theory 錯誤理論**: Moral statements are truth-apt but systematically false (Mackie)

### Non-Cognitivism 非認知主義
Moral statements do not express truth-apt beliefs but rather emotions, prescriptions, or attitudes.
道德陳述不是命題，而是態度或情感表達。

**Varieties 分支**:
| Theory | Key Claim | Key Thinker |
|:-------|:----------|:------------|
| **Emotivism 情緒論** | "X is wrong" = "Boo X!" | A.J. Ayer, C.L. Stevenson |
| **Prescriptivism 規約論** | Moral judgments are universal prescriptions | R.M. Hare |
| **Expressivism 表達主義** | Moral language expresses non-cognitive attitudes | Allan Gibbard, Simon Blackburn |
| **Quasi-Realism 準實在論** | We can talk *as if* moral realism is true | Simon Blackburn |

## 道德實在論 / Moral Realism

Moral realism holds that there are objective moral facts, independent of human opinion.
道德實在論主張存在獨立於人心的客觀道德事實。

### Naturalism 自然主義
Moral properties are identical to or reducible to natural properties.
- **Cornell Realism**: Richard Boyd, Nicholas Sturgeon — moral properties are natural kinds
- **Analytic Naturalism**: "Good" = "maximizes pleasure" (utilitarian naturalism)

### Non-Naturalism 非自然主義
Moral properties are sui generis, irreducible to natural properties.
- **G.E. Moore**: *Principia Ethica* (1903) — the "naturalistic fallacy"
  - The **Open Question Argument 開放問題論證**: For any natural property N, it remains an open
    question whether something with N is "good." Therefore, "good" ≠ N.
- **Intuitionism 直覺主義**: W.D. Ross — we grasp moral truths via rational intuition

## 錯誤理論 / Error Theory

J.L. Mackie's error theory (*Ethics: Inventing Right and Wrong*, 1977):

**Two Arguments 兩大論證**:
1. **Argument from Relativity 來自相對性的論證**: Moral codes vary radically across cultures.
   This is better explained by social construction than by objective moral facts.
2. **Argument from Queerness 來自怪異性的論證**: If objective moral facts existed, they would be
   "entities or qualities or relations of a very strange sort, utterly different from anything
   else in the universe" — and would require a "special faculty" to perceive them.

**Implication**: All moral claims are false (error theory), but we can continue using moral
language as a useful fiction.

## 道德知識論 / Moral Epistemology

| Approach | Description | Key Thinkers |
|:---------|:------------|:-------------|
| **Ethical Intuitionism** | We grasp basic moral truths through rational intuition | Moore, Ross, Audi |
| **Coherentism 融貫論** | Moral beliefs justified by coherence with our broader web of beliefs | Rawls (reflective equilibrium) |
| **Moral Perception** | We literally *perceive* right and wrong | Audi, McBrayer |
| **Evolutionary Debunking** | Evolution explains moral beliefs without reference to moral truth | Street, Joyce |

## 比較表 / Comparison Table

| Position | Truth-Apt? | Objective Facts? | Moral Knowledge Possible? |
|:---------|:----------:|:----------------:|:-------------------------:|
| Naturalist Realism | Yes | Yes (natural facts) | Yes |
| Non-Naturalist Realism | Yes | Yes (sui generis) | Yes (intuition) |
| Error Theory | Yes (but all false) | No | No |
| Emotivism | No | No | No (no truth) |
| Expressivism | No | No | N/A |

## 當代討論 / Contemporary Debates

- **Moral Twin Earth** (Terence Horgan & Mark Timmons): A challenge to naturalist moral realism
- **Disagreement & Convergence**: Does persistent moral disagreement undermine realism?
- **Companions in Guilt**: If mathematical realism is defensible, why not moral realism?
