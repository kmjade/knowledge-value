# 倫理學學習路徑 / Ethics Learning Pathway

A structured 4-week self-study plan for DDC 170-倫理學.
四周結構化自學計劃，適合初學者系統掌握倫理學核心內容。

## 學習前提 / Prerequisites

- Basic familiarity with philosophical reasoning 基本哲學推理能力
- Recommended: [[../../100-总论/README|100 總論]] (metaphysics, free will)
- Recommended: [[../../120-认识论/README|120 認識論]] (for moral epistemology)

---

## Week 1: Foundations — 基礎層

### Day 1–2: Meta-Ethics 元倫理學
- Read: [[../01-元倫理學/01-元倫理學|01 元倫理學]]
- **Key Questions**: Are there objective moral truths? What does "good" mean?
- **Core Reading**: Mackie's "argument from queerness," Moore's open question argument
- **Exercise**: Classify statements as cognitivist or non-cognitivist

### Day 3–4: Deontology 義務論
- Read: [[../02-義務論/02-義務論|02 義務論]]
- **Key Questions**: Can lying ever be right? What is the categorical imperative?
- **Core Reading**: Kant's *Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals*, Sections 1–2
- **Exercise**: Apply the three formulations of the categorical imperative to a real dilemma

### Day 5–7: Review & Integration
- Re-read: [[../00-MOCs/倫理學知識庫總覽|知識庫總覽]]
- Compare deontology with meta-ethical foundations
- **Writing Prompt**: "Is the categorical imperative a test for moral truth, or just consistency?"

---

## Week 2: Core Normative Theories — 核心規範理論

### Day 8–9: Utilitarianism 功利主義
- Read: [[../03-功利主義/03-功利主義|03 功利主義]]
- **Key Questions**: Can we measure happiness? Is the utility monster a fatal objection?
- **Core Reading**: Mill's *Utilitarianism*, Chapters 2 & 4
- **Exercise**: Calculate a simple hedonic calculus for a policy decision

### Day 10–11: Virtue Ethics 德性倫理
- Read: [[../04-德性倫理/04-德性倫理|04 德性倫理]]
- **Key Questions**: What is eudaimonia? Can virtues be taught?
- **Core Reading**: Aristotle's *Nicomachean Ethics*, Books I–II
- **Exercise**: Identify the virtues relevant to a professional role

### Day 12–14: Theory Comparison
- Create a comparison chart: deontology vs utilitarianism vs virtue ethics
- **Writing Prompt**: "Which normative theory best handles the problem of moral luck?"

---

## Week 3: Context and Application — 脈絡與應用

### Day 15–16: Contractarianism 契約論
- Read: [[../05-契約論/05-契約論|05 契約論]]
- **Key Questions**: Why would rational agents agree to moral constraints? What is the original position?
- **Core Reading**: Rawls' *A Theory of Justice*, Sections 3–4
- **Exercise**: Design principles from behind a "veil of ignorance"

### Day 17–18: Applied Ethics 應用倫理
- Read: [[../06-應用倫理/06-應用倫理|06 應用倫理]]
- **Key Questions**: How do theories handle the trolley problem? What about autonomous weapons?
- **Core Reading**: Thomson's "The Trolley Problem", Sparrow on autonomous weapons
- **Exercise**: Analyze a bioethics case using all three normative theories

### Day 19–21: Moral Psychology 道德心理學
- Read: [[../07-道德心理學/07-道德心理學|07 道德心理學]]
- **Key Questions**: Is moral judgment rational or emotional? How does moral development occur?
- **Core Reading**: Greene et al. (2001) fMRI trolley study, Haidt's "The Emotional Dog"
- **Exercise**: Reflect on a personal moral dilemma using dual-process theory

---

## Week 4: Advanced Topics & Integration — 進階與整合

### Day 22–23: Meta-Normative Theory 後設倫理
- Read: [[../08-後設倫理/08-後設倫理|08 後設倫理]]
- **Key Questions**: Must moral judgments motivate? Does morality supervene on the natural?
- **Core Reading**: Smith's *The Moral Problem*, Dancy's *Moral Particularism*
- **Exercise**: Debate internalism vs externalism

### Day 24–25: Comparative Ethics 比較倫理
- Read: [[../09-比較倫理/09-比較倫理|09 比較倫理]]
- **Key Questions**: Is Confucian ethics a form of virtue ethics? How does Buddhist ethics differ from Western?
- **Core Reading**: *Analects* selections, Shantideva's *Bodhicaryavatara*, Gilligan's *In a Different Voice*
- **Exercise**: Compare Confucian *ren* with Aristotelian *phronesis*

### Day 26–28: Final Integration 最終整合
- Synthesize a personal ethical framework drawing from all theories
- **Capstone Prompt**: "Construct your own normative theory. Defend it against objections from rival approaches."
- Review all [[../99-資源收集/倫理學資源收集|資源收集]] for further reading

---

## 推薦學習節奏 / Recommended Pace

- **Daily**: 60–90 minutes reading + 30 minutes note-taking
- **Weekly**: One 2-hour discussion/reflection session
- **Tools**: Obsidian or similar for bidirectional linking
