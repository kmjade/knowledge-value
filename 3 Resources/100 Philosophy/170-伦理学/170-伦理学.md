# 170 倫理學 / Ethics

Ethics (from Greek *ethos*, "character") is the branch of philosophy that systematizes,
defends, and recommends concepts of right and wrong conduct. 倫理學研究道德的本質、
來源與應用。

## 三大規範倫理理論比較 / Three Normative Theories Compared

| Dimension | Deontology 義務論 | Utilitarianism 功利主義 | Virtue Ethics 德性倫理 |
|:----------|:------------------|:------------------------|:-----------------------|
| **Focus** | Duty, rules, intentions | Consequences, welfare | Character, flourishing |
| **Key Question** | What is my duty? | What maximizes well-being? | What would a virtuous person do? |
| **Core Concept** | Categorical Imperative | Greatest Happiness Principle | Eudaimonia, Phronesis |
| **Strengths** | Respects persons as ends | Impartial, practical | Holistic, motivational |
| **Weaknesses** | Rigidity, conflicting duties | Demandingness, justice concerns | Vagueness, no action guidance |
| **Key Thinkers** | Kant, Ross, Korsgaard | Bentham, Mill, Singer | Aristotle, Anscombe, Hursthouse |

## 核心問題 / Core Questions

1. **Normative**: What ought we to do? 我們應當做什麼？
2. **Meta-ethical**: What does "good" even mean? 「善」究竟意味著什麼？
3. **Applied**: How do principles apply to real cases? 原則如何應用於具體案例？
4. **Psychological**: How do we actually make moral judgments? 我們實際上如何做道德判斷？
